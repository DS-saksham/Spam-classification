What is Vectorization?
-- Vectorization is the process of converting texual data into numerical format, specially vectors.
The goal of vectorization is to transform the original text into a sturctured format(vector) that 
cpatures the relevant information form the text while retaining as much meaning as possible.

## Steps to transform text into usable numerical representation include::
1. Capturing the relationship between words and the context in which they appear
2. Maintaining the structure and patterns inherent in the text
3. Reducing dimensionality.

## Common Vectorization Methods:: 
1.Bag-of-Words (BoW) Model:
-- Simplest and most common used method.
-- It works by representating text as a bag(set) of words, where the order of the words is igonored 
-- but the frequency is counted.
How this works:
1. Vocabulary Creation: The entire corpus (collection of documents) is scanned, and all unique words (tokens) are collected in a vocabulary
2.Vector Representation: Each document is then represented as a vector with the size equal to the number of words in the vocabulary. The value of each element in the vector is the count of that wordâ€™s occurrences in the document.

Limitation:
1.Sparsity: BoW creates very sparse vectors (most entries are 0s), especially with large vocabularies.
2.Ignores Word Order: BoW doesn't capture the meaning based on the order or sequence of words
3.No Semantic Meaning: It treats all words equally, regardless of their meaning or relationships.
